Do you know that the first helicopter has just landed on Mars? Recently, A new Mars rover called "Perseverance" touched down in Jezero Crater.

This rover is the fifth robotic rover that the Nasa has sent to Mars. The nuclear battery-powered rover, which is about the size of a small car, is designed to take 5 soil samples, store them in containers, and deposit them on the surface of Mars so that a future mission can return them to Earth.

However, this rover will not be alone. This time, there's a helicopter mounted on the rover! It's called the Ingenuity. It's a small remote helicopter that is about as heavy as a bowling ball. The most amazing part of this helicopete: The helicopter flies on its own, without human control! The Mars atmosphere is 99% thinner than regular Earth atmospher. So the blades are much bigger and the spinning speed is alot higher. It also uses solar power to keep the rover charged.

I think these are all great breakthrough in science. I hope these will help scientist farther more.
